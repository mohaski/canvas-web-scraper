# Activity 3: Executive Summary Sprint

## **Why Executive Summaries Are a Core Analyst Skill**

The executive summary is one of the highest-leverage documents a data professional can produce. A well-written executive summary can get an entire analysis read, or it can result in the work being ignored. In professional settings, you will often have less than 90 seconds of an executive's attention. This activity forces you to be ruthless about what matters.

## **The Scenario**

|  |
| --- |
| **Context**  You work as a data analyst for the Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA). Your team has completed a 3-month analysis of small business compliance with digital payment regulations introduced in January 2024.     Key findings from your analysis (you may interpret and frame these, but do not change the numbers):     • 71% of small businesses in Nairobi now use at least one registered digital payment method (up from 43% pre-regulation)  • Compliance is significantly lower in peri-urban areas: 38% compliance in towns of 50,000–200,000 residents  • Non-compliant businesses cite two primary barriers: setup cost (68% of respondents) and lack of awareness of the regulation (54%)  • Enforcement actions have been taken against 1,247 businesses — 89% subsequently came into compliance within 60 days  • Projected tax revenue uplift from full compliance: KSh 4.2 billion annually     Your audience: The Commissioner General of KRA, who will brief the Cabinet Secretary for Finance. |

 

## **Your Task: Write a 220-Word Executive Summary**

|  |
| --- |
| **Format Requirements: Every section is mandatory**  Use the following structure exactly (label each section in your submission):     HEADLINE (1 sentence, max 20 words): The single most important finding, stated plainly.     SITUATION (2–3 sentences): What was studied, why it matters, and the key context.     KEY FINDINGS (3 bullet points): The three most decision-relevant findings. Each bullet: one sentence, plain language, includes the most important number.     RECOMMENDATION (2–3 sentences): What should be done next? Be specific — not 'increase awareness' but 'launch a 90-day awareness campaign targeting peri-urban counties with compliance rates below 50%.'     RISK / CAVEAT (1 sentence): What is the most important limitation of this analysis?     TOTAL TARGET: 180–220 words. Being significantly over or under will result in a fail. |

 

## **Pass/Fail Checklist**

Your Executive Summary earns a PASS when it meets ALL of the following:

1. Has all 5 sections labeled (HEADLINE, SITUATION, KEY FINDINGS, RECOMMENDATION, RISK/CAVEAT)
2. Is between 180–220 words (not counting section labels)
3. HEADLINE is a single sentence that contains the most important finding
4. KEY FINDINGS contains exactly 3 bullets, each with at least one specific number
5. RECOMMENDATION is specific and actionable (not generic)
6. Contains zero statistical jargon (no p-values, confidence intervals, regression terms)
7. Does not contradict any data point given in the scenario

 

|  |
| --- |
| **Pro Tip**  Write your draft first without worrying about word count. Then cut ruthlessly, every sentence that does not directly serve the decision-maker is a candidate for removal. The discipline of the word limit is the point of the exercise. |
