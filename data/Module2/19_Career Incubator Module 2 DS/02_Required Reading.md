# Required Reading

|  |
| --- |
| **Article**  Cole Nussbaumer Knaflic, "Choosing an Effective Visual," in Storytelling with Data (Wiley, 2015), Chapter 2.  If you do not have the book, a freely available summary is posted in the LMS resource library under Module 2 > Readings.  Focus on: How does the type of message (comparison, trend, distribution, relationship) map to the chart type? Note at least two examples that resonate with your own analytical work. |

 

## **Required Video**

|  |
| --- |
| **Video (~12 minutes)**  Hans Rosling, "The Best Stats You've Ever Seen" (TED, 2006). Available on TED.com and YouTube.  As you watch, consider: What storytelling techniques does Rosling use? How does he handle audience unfamiliarity with the data? What emotional arc does he create? You will reference this in Activity 1. |

 

## **Reflection**

Before moving to Activity 1, take 5 minutes to write (in your notes) your answer to:

|  |
| --- |
| **Quick Reflection**  "Think of a time when you tried to share a data-based insight with someone, a friend, a teacher, a family member, and they did not understand it. What happened? What did you wish you had done differently?"  This is preparation for the thinking you will do in Activity 1. |
