Here are two examples from different industries showcasing how means and standard deviations are used to solve business problems.

### Insurance Company - Setting Premiums (Financial Services)

![Close up of front side of a white automobile](https://moringa.instructure.com/courses/1406/files/625064/download)

**Business Problem:**

An insurance company needs to set fair and profitable premiums for car insurance. They have data on past claims, including the cost of repairs and medical bills.

**Data Science Solution:**

* The company calculates the average cost (mean) of claims for different car models, driver demographics, and locations.
* They then calculate the standard deviation to understand how much the claim amounts vary around the average.
* This helps them set premiums that reflect the risk profile of each driver. Drivers with a higher risk of filing expensive claims (farther from the mean) will pay a higher premium.

### Retail Store - Optimizing Inventory Management (Retail)

![Clothing retail store](https://moringa.instructure.com/courses/1406/files/624987/download)

**Business Problem:**

A retail store struggles with stockouts (running out of popular items) and excess inventory (items sitting unsold). This leads to lost sales and wasted storage space.

**Data Science Solution:**

* The store analyzes historical sales data to calculate the average demand (mean) for each product throughout the year.
* They use the standard deviation to understand the natural fluctuation in demand. This helps them set safety stock levels to avoid stockouts during peak periods.
* By considering both the average and the variability (standard deviation) in demand, the store can optimize inventory levels and improve sales efficiency.
