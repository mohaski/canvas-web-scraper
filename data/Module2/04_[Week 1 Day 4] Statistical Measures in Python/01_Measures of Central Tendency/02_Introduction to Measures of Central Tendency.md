In the realm of statistics, central tendency, also referred to as a measure of central tendency, serves a critical role. It summarizes a dataset by pinpointing a single value that represents its central location. This value reflects where most of the data points tend to cluster within the distribution.

While the term "mean” or “average" might be familiar, central tendency encompasses a broader concept. There are, in fact, several statistical measures that can be used to capture the notion of **central location**: each with its own advantages and limitations. Beyond the mean, some very important and commonly used summary statistics of this type are the **median** and **mode**. The choice of which measure to employ depends on the specific characteristics of your data and the nature of the question you're seeking to answer. This section will delve into the different types of measures used to capture central tendency, equipping you to select the most appropriate statistic for your data and analysis.
