You learned about some commonly used measures of dispersion. These include the MAD, the standard deviation, and the IQR.

Recall that these were defined as:

**Standard Deviation (SD):** This is a common measure of how spread out data is from the average (mean). It considers how far each data point deviates from the mean, squared (to account for direction), and then averages those squared deviations and square roots this average. However, SD can be heavily influenced by extreme values (outliers) in the data.

**Mean Absolute Deviation (MAD):** This is a more robust measure of spread than standard deviation. MAD calculates the mean of the absolute deviations from the man.

**Interquartile Range (IQR):** IQR is the difference between the third quartile (Q3) and the first quartile (Q1). So, it represents the range that covers the middle half of the data.

You will be using these statistics along with measures of central tendency like the mean, median, mode to provide high-level summaries of your data and its distribution. Once in hand, these statistics can be used to assess the structure of your data, identify groups, conduct comparisons between them, and help make generalizable inferences that are relevant to the problem you are tackling.
