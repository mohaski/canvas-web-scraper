### Pharmaceutical Research

**![several unmarked white pills on a surface](https://moringa.instructure.com/courses/1406/files/624731/download)
Business Problem**

Pharmaceutical research relies heavily on different measures of dispersion to analyze data from clinical trials. Imagine a drug targeting blood pressure. Standard deviation is crucial here. It reveals how consistently the drug lowers blood pressure across participants. A high standard deviation indicates a large spread in responses, suggesting the drug might not work uniformly for everyone. This variation could be due to individual differences in genetics or pre-existing conditions, or it could hint at potential side effects for some patients.

**Solution**

Interquartile Range complements this analysis by focusing on the middle 50% of the data, excluding outliers. This reveals how much the drug typically affects blood pressure for most participants. By examining both standard deviation and interquartile range alongside the average blood pressure change, researchers gain a richer understanding of the drug's effectiveness. They can identify potential side effects in individuals with extreme responses and refine dosing strategies to achieve consistent results with minimal side effects for the broader patient population. Ultimately, these measures of dispersion play a key role in ensuring the safety and efficacy of new medications.
