Data science is the art and method of extracting knowledge and insights from data using computational and statistical methods. It is a hybrid field leveraging vast amounts of data collected by organizations to aid decision-making and fuel new data-driven products. At its core, data science involves sophisticated data management tools, computer-aided experimentation, and statistical learning techniques. Let's break down the key components of data science, both as a subject matter and as a process.

### The Four Pillars of Data Science

Data Science is comprised of four important pillars. Each of the pillars provides a part of the foundational knowledge and skills needed for success in the field.

![Four pillars of data science: domain knowledge, statistical skills, computer science, communication and visualization](https://moringa.instructure.com/courses/1406/files/625162/download)

1. **Domain Knowledge:** This pillar ensures that the insights generated are relevant to the specific field and problem at hand. Domain knowledge provides the context and meaning to the data, making it possible to address real-world issues effectively.
2. **Statistics and Probability:** This pillar provides the foundation for understanding data. It involves analyzing characteristic values, assessing trends and relationships, and determining if the data divides meaningfully into subgroups. Understanding the spread and uncertainty in measured quantities is crucial for making informed decisions.
3. **Computational Methods:** This pillar focuses on programming, data manipulation and retrieval, and task automation (algorithms). It enables predictive tasks and insights based on automated pattern recognition. The intersection of statistical principles and computer science, known as **machine learning**, is central to this pillar.
4. **Communication:** This pillar emphasizes the importance of effectively communicating complex findings to stakeholders, often without a technical background. Clear presentations, reports, and visualizations are essential to ensure that insights are understood and actionable.

### Summary

Data science is a multidisciplinary field leveraging statistics, computational methods, domain knowledge, and communication to turn raw data into actionable insights. By understanding and applying these four pillars, data scientists can address diverse challenges across multiple industries.
