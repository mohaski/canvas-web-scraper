SQL queries can quickly become complex, especially when dealing with intricate relationships between multiple tables. In the previous lab, you encountered a many-to-many join that required joining four different tables—a challenging task that might have felt overwhelming. This scenario is just the beginning; in real-world databases, the complexity can increase significantly. For instance, you may need to join subsets of data from various tables or even incorporate multiple layers of joins to retrieve the necessary information.

When queries reach this level of complexity, it can be difficult to manage and understand the logic all at once. This is where the concept of subqueries becomes invaluable. Subqueries allow you to break down a large, complex query into smaller, more manageable parts. By isolating specific portions of the query, you can focus on one task at a time, ensuring accuracy and making the overall query easier to construct and debug.

Subqueries can serve various purposes, such as filtering data, performing calculations, or even simplifying the structure of your main query. They act as building blocks that contribute to a more understandable and efficient SQL query.

This topic covers:

* Writing Subqueries: Decomposing complex SQL queries into smaller, more digestible tasks by using subqueries. This skill will help you handle more sophisticated database operations with greater ease and precision
