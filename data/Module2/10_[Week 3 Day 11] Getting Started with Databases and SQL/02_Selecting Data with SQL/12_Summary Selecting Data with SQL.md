SQL (Structured Query Language) is the industry-standard language for interacting with structured databases, which are digital tools designed for efficient storage and retrieval of large-scale information. These databases facilitate the creation and discovery of relationships between various data entities.

The process of working with SQL databases typically involves establishing a connection, writing a query, and executing it to retrieve data. SQL queries often follow a specific structure, represented by the mnemonic **"Sweaty Feet Will Give Horrible Odors,"** which stands for **Select, From, Where, Group By, Having, and Order By**. This structured approach allows users to efficiently extract, manipulate, and analyze data stored in relational databases.
