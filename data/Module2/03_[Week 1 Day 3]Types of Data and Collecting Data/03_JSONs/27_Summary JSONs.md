JSONs are quite flexible. This is a virtue – they have the flexibility to encode data and relationships that can be more complex than what is allowed by tabular representation. You have seen this yourself when noting that a given dictionary within the hierarchy of JSON could possess both primitive datatypes (strings and numeric entries) as well as additional collections (lists, dicts, tuples). But it also makes certain things more difficult. Parsing JSON text from scratch can be annoying or even difficult. Thankfully, the parsing and data type handling is made quite a bit easier by the json module.

However, the trickier part of dealing with JSONs still remains: understanding the way your data is organized and extracting relevant information from what are often deeply nested structures. You've seen how to do this in the technical walkthrough. It involves a lot of snooping around Python data structures, type checking, and using looping or comprehensions to get the data you need out.

While this all might seem like a chore at times, the ability to navigate these complex structures becomes an invaluable asset as you progress in data science. Mastering JSON manipulation equips you to unlock a vast ecosystem of data sources, from APIs to social media feeds to scientific research. As you build your data science expertise, this skill will empower you to transform diverse data formats into valuable insights, ultimately driving informed decision-making across various domains.
