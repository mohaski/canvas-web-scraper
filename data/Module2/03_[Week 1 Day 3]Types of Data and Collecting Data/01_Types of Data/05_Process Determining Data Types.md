For a beginning data science student, understanding data types is crucial. Here's a process to determine the type of data:

1. **Examine the data:** Look at the values in your dataset.
2. **Consider the nature of the data:**
   1. Can it be counted as whole numbers?
   2. Does it represent measurements that can have fractional values?
   3. Does it represent categories or labels?
   4. Do these categories have a meaningful order?
3. **Categorize based on these observations:**
   1. Integer numeric: Whole numbers (e.g., count of items, age in years)
   2. Continuous numeric: Numbers that can have fractional values (e.g., height, weight, temperature)
   3. Nominal categorical: Labels with no inherent order (e.g., color, gender, blood type)
   4. Ordinal categorical: Categories with a meaningful order (e.g., education level, customer satisfaction ratings)
4. **Consider the context:** Sometimes, the same data could be interpreted differently based on its use in your analysis.
5. **Verify your classification:** Double-check your determination, as it will affect how you handle the data in your analysis.
