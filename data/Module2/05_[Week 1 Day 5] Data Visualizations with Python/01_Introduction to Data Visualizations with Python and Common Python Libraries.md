* How to identify different data types (numerical, categorical, etc.) and their best visual representations

### Industry Example

#### Marketing & Sales

**Clear Business Problem**

A marketing team for a clothing retailer struggles to understand how different age groups interact with their brand and what products resonate most with each demographic. This lack of insight makes it difficult to tailor marketing messages and product offerings for maximum impact.

**Concise Overview**

Data visualization comes to the rescue! By creating bar charts that segment customer purchases by age group, the marketing team can clearly see which age groups favor specific clothing styles or price ranges. This allows them to develop targeted marketing campaigns with visuals and messaging that resonate with each customer segment.

#### Sports Industry

**Clear Business Problem**

The performance analysts for a professional basketball team are drowning in player data, making it difficult to identify trends and develop effective game strategies. They need a way to quickly analyze and interpret complex player performance metrics.

**Concise Overview**

Data visualization steps up to the challenge! Analysts can leverage scatter plots to visualize the relationship between a player's shot attempts from different areas of the court and their shooting success rate. This allows them to identify areas where a player excels or struggles, informing targeted practice drills and strategic adjustments during games.

### Learning Outcomes

After completing this lesson, you should be able to:

* Recall the definitions of continuous and discrete numeric variables and apply this knowledge to select appropriate visualization techniques.
* Gain proficiency in core Matplotlib functions to construct bar charts, line charts, scatter plots, and more in order to reproduce various chart types in Python.
* Apply data visualization techniques learned in Matplotlib to create informative visual representations of data
* Apply customization techniques to enhance the clarity and aesthetics of data visualizations, including customizing labels, titles, colors, and legends.
* Articulate and apply data visualization best practices. These include choosing the right chart type for the data, using clear and concise labels, and ensuring accessibility for all viewers.
