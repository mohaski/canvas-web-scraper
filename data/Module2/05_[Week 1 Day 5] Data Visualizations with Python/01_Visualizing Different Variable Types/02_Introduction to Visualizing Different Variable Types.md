In today's data world, understanding how to visually represent information is key. This session focuses on the art of visualizing **data types**, the categories your data falls into. There are two main categories: **numeric data** and **categorical data**. Numeric data deals with numbers, while categorical data sorts information into distinct groups or labels (like product category: clothing, electronics, etc.).

But within numeric data, there are further distinctions that impact how we visualize it. We'll delve into two crucial types: **discrete** and **continuous**. Discrete numeric variables represent whole numbers you can count (like the number of website orders), while continuous variables encompass any value within a range (like customer age). Imagine being a marketing analyst*—*understanding these distinctions is crucial. Choosing the right visual for discrete data (like bar charts) versus continuous data (like histograms) makes a big difference in effectively communicating your findings. By mastering this skill, you'll be able to create impactful visualizations that unlock the stories hidden within your data.
