This topic introduces the two fundamental data structures that form the backbone of Pandas: Series and DataFrames. We will explore how to load data in files into these structures using functions like pd.read\_csv (for CSV files). We will also look at techniques to gain a high level understanding of the data (i.e. its schema) by inspecting column names, data types, and computing summary statistics.

Then we'll go over how Series and DataFrames are structured and how to use pandas accessors to select specific subsets of the data that you are interested in. We will see that beyond simple integer-based positional indexing, you can use label-based indexing and even logical conditions for accessing the rows and columns relevant to your analysis.

And finally, we'll delve into methods for modifying your data. This includes techniques for changing data values directly, altering data indexing (the labels associated with rows or columns), and transforming subsets of data using native and custom functions.
