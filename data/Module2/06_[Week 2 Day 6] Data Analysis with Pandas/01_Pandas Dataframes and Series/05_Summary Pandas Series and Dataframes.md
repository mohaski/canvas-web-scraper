You have been introduced to the Pandas library and its foundational data structures*—*the Series and DataFrame.

* The **DataFrame** is a structure optimized for tabular data manipulation.
* The **Series** is a one-dimensional array that is a hybrid between a dictionary and a numpy array that sports a host of vectorized methods for data manipulation and cleaning.

Together, these data structures and their associated methods can be used to inspect, clean, and transform a given dataset to a form suitable for later analysis. What we covered in this topic was just the basics of this entire process: loading data into DataFrames, getting a high level overview of your DataFrame, setting and modifying your row and column indices, selecting subsets of data and transforming these subsets using vectorized methods. In other topics, we will use what we have learned here to dig deeper into data cleaning, transformation, and analysis.
