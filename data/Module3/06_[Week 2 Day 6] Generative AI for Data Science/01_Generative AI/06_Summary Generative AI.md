### Terms Recap

* **Generative AI:** AI systems that create new content from learned patterns.
* **LLMs (Large Language Models):** Neural networks trained on vast text datasets.
* **Tokens:** Basic units of text/code that AI processes.
* **Parameters:** Variables determining AI model behavior.
* **Fine-tuning:** Process of specializing models for specific tasks.
* **Prompts:** Input text guiding AI generation.

### Key Concepts

Generative AI processes input through neural networks, predicts likely outputs based on learned patterns, and generates coherent responses while maintaining context. It functions like an advanced pattern recognition and prediction system, capable of understanding and generating human-like content.

**Practical Applications:**

* Code generation and debugging
* Data analysis and cleaning
* Content creation and documentation
* Feature engineering in data science
* Report generation and summarization

The technology serves as a collaborative tool, enhancing productivity while requiring human oversight to ensure accuracy and appropriateness of outputs. Success depends on clear problem definition, well-structured prompts, and careful validation of results.

### Brief Process Overview

1. **Problem Definition & Input Preparation:**  
   * Define requirements and gather context.
   * Structure clear prompts.
2. **Initial Generation & Review:**  
   * Generate and assess initial output.
   * Identify improvement areas.
3. **Refinement & Iteration:**  
   * Adjust prompts and regenerate.
   * Fine-tune based on feedback.
4. **Validation & Testing:**  
   * Verify accuracy and functionality.
   * Test against requirements.
5. **Implementation & Documentation:**  
   * Integrate validated content.
   * Document process and decisions.
