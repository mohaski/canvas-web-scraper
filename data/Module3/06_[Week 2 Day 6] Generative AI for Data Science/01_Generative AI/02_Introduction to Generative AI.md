Generative AI refers to AI systems that can create new content (text, code, images, etc.) based on training data and user prompts. Common examples include ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini.

In a typical workplace scenario, generative AI could be used in the following situations:

* A marketing manager might use it to draft email campaigns or social media posts.
* A developer could use it to explain code, debug issues, or generate boilerplate code.
* A data analyst might use it to help write SQL queries or explain statistical concepts.

Key generative AI concepts:

* **Prompts:** Instructions you give the AI.
* **Tokens:** Units of text the AI processes.
* **Context window:** How much information the AI can consider at once.
* **Fine-tuning:** Customizing AI models for specific tasks.

The main value is accelerating routine tasks while letting humans focus on strategy and refinement. For example, rather than starting a document from scratch, you might use AI to generate an initial draft and then edit it.
