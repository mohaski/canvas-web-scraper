You are a junior data scientist at a retail company that manages several stores selling a wide range of products. Your team is responsible for analyzing sales data to derive insights that inform business strategies, such as product promotions, inventory management, and customer engagement. The sales data includes details such as product names, quantities sold, customer information, and prices.

Recently, there has been a focus on understanding the sales trends for specific product types, such as gaming and desk-related products, which have shown patterns of being purchased together. Additionally, the company aims to identify high-value customers and popular products to maximize revenue and improve marketing efforts.

**Challenge:** The primary challenge is to derive actionable insights from large datasets using a combination of PySpark and SQL operations. This includes filtering and transforming the data, calculating key metrics such as revenue and quantity sold, and visualizing results for easy interpretation.

By completing this practice, you will reinforce your skills in:

* Data loading, cleaning, and transformation using PySpark.
* Executing complex SQL queries for data aggregation and analysis on distributed data.
* Creating meaningful visualizations to present insights derived from data analysis.
* Applying these skills to solve industry-standard problems related to product performance and customer engagement in a retail context.

This practice will provide valuable experience in using PySpark and SQL together to analyze and interpret sales data, skills that are critical for data-driven decision-making in business settings.

### Step 2: Clean and Preprocess the Data

```
# Filter out rows with null values in critical columns  
cleaned_df = sales_df.filter(  
    sales_df["customer_id"].isNotNull() &   
    sales_df["product_id"].isNotNull() &   
    sales_df["product_price"].isNotNull()  
)  
  
# Remove duplicates based on 'sale_id'  
cleaned_df = cleaned_df.dropDuplicates(["sale_id"])
```

### Step 3: Register the Cleaned DataFrame as a Temporary SQL Table

```
# Register the DataFrame as a temporary SQL table  
cleaned_df.createOrReplaceTempView("sales_table")
```

### Step 4: Analyze Total Revenue per Product and Identify the Top 5 Products by Type for Particular Parameters

```
# Use SQL to calculate total revenue per product  
subscription_summary = spark.sql("""  
   SELECT product_name, SUM(product_price * quantity) AS total_revenue  
   FROM sales_table  
   WHERE product_name LIKE 'Gaming%' OR product_name LIKE 'Desk%'   
   GROUP BY product_name  
   ORDER BY total_revenue DESC  
   LIMIT 5  
""")  
subscription_summary.show()
```

