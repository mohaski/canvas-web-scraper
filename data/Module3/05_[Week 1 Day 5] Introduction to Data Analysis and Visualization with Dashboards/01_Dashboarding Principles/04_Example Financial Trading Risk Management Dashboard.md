You're a junior data analyst at a large investment bank. The Risk Management department has identified a need for a more comprehensive and real-time view of trading activities and associated risks across various asset classes. The Chief Risk Officer (CRO) has tasked you with creating a dashboard to monitor and analyze trading risk in real-time.

**Problem:** The risk management team lacks a unified, real-time view of trading positions, market exposures, and potential risks across different trading desks. This makes it challenging to quickly identify and respond to emerging risks, ensure compliance with risk limits, and provide timely insights to senior management. The CRO needs a tool to monitor key risk metrics, analyze exposure patterns, and facilitate rapid decision-making in volatile market conditions.

**Objective:** Create a comprehensive trading risk management dashboard that enables the risk management team and senior executives to:

* Monitor real-time trading positions and associated risks across asset classes.
* Track key risk metrics such as Value at Risk (VaR), exposure limits, and liquidity ratios.
* Analyze risk concentration and correlation across different portfolios.
* Identify potential regulatory compliance issues.

#### Step 1. Define Dashboard Objectives

**Primary audience:** Risk Management team and senior executives (CRO, CFO)

**Key questions to answer:**

* What is our current risk exposure across different asset classes?
* Are we within our risk limits?
* How are market conditions affecting our risk profile?
* Are there any emerging risks or anomalies

#### Step 2. Gather and Prepare Data

* **Sources:** Trading systems, market data feeds, risk models.
* **Data needed:** Real-time trading positions, market prices, volatility data, correlation matrices.
* **Preparation:** Aggregate data by asset class and trading desk, calculate risk metrics.

#### Step 3. Define Metrics and KPIs

* Value at Risk (VaR) by asset class and trading desk
* Risk-weighted assets (RWA)
* Exposure limits and utilization percentages
* Liquidity coverage ratio
* Sharpe ratio for risk-adjusted returns
* Stress test results

#### Step 4. Select Visuals

* **Heat map or Bar chart:** For risk exposure across asset classes
* **Line chart:** VaR trends over time
* **Treemap or Stacked bar:** Portfolio composition and risk contribution
* **Gauge charts/Bar charts:** Limit utilization
* **Network diagram (non-data):** Counterparty risk visualization

#### Step 5. Design Layout

* **Apply F-pattern:**  
  + Top left: Overall VaR and limit utilization
  + Top right: Key risk alerts
  + Middle: Heat map of risk exposure
  + Bottom left: VaR trend chart
  + Bottom right: Portfolio composition treemap
* **Color scheme:**  
  + Base: Bank's blue and gold colors
  + Risk levels: Green (low), Yellow (moderate), Red (high)

#### Step 6. Develop Dashboard

* Implement chosen visualizations
* Ensure real-time data updates
* Add interactivity:  
  + Filters for asset class and trading desk
  + Time range selector
  + Drill-down capabilities
* Hover tooltips for detailed information

#### Step 7. Deploy and Maintain

* Set up secure access for authorized personnel.
* Implement real-time data feeds.
* Establish monitoring for dashboard performance and data accuracy.

#### Step 8. Document and Refine

* Create user guide and data dictionary.
* Conduct training sessions for risk management team.
* Gather feedback through user interviews and surveys.
* Iterate on design based on user input and changing risk management needs.

### Walkthrough of User Experience

The following walkthrough demonstrates how the dashboard enables the risk management team to quickly identify, analyze, and respond to emerging risks, fulfilling the objectives set out at the beginning of the process.

1. **Dashboard Overview:** The risk manager opens the dashboard and immediately sees the overall VaR figure prominently displayed, color-coded to indicate the current risk level.
2. **Risk Exposure Analysis:** They scan the heat map, which shows risk exposure across different asset classes. They notice a particularly dark red area in emerging market equities.
3. **Drill-Down:** Clicking on the emerging market equities cell in the heat map, the dashboard updates to show detailed metrics for this asset class.
4. **Trend Analysis:** The risk manager examines the VaR trend line chart, noticing a sharp increase over the past week for emerging market equities.
5. **Limit Check:** They check the gauge charts for limit utilization, seeing that the emerging market equity exposure is nearing its limit.
6. **Portfolio Composition:** The treemap shows that a significant portion of the risk in emerging market equities is concentrated in a few specific countries.
7. **Counterparty Risk:** The network diagram reveals that a large portion of this risk is tied to a single counterparty.
8. **Action:** Based on this analysis, the risk manager decides to alert the trading desk about the concentration risk and recommend reducing exposure to the identified counterparty.
9. **Scenario Analysis:** Before making the recommendation, they use the built-in scenario analysis tool to model the impact of reducing this exposure on the overall risk profile.
10. **Reporting:** Finally, they use the dashboard's reporting feature to generate a summary for the upcoming risk committee meeting, highlighting the identified risk and proposed mitigation strategy.
