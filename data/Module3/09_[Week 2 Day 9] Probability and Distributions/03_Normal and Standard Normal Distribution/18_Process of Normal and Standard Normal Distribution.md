### Step 1: Verify Normal Distribution Appropriateness

Before applying normal distribution concepts, we need to confirm our data is reasonably normal or can be approximated by a normal distribution. This involves:

* Examining the data's shape through visualization
* Checking for symmetry around a central value
* Verifying that most observations cluster around the mean
* Considering the Central Limit Theorem if we're working with sample means

### Step 2: Characterize the Distribution

Once we've confirmed normality, we need to understand our specific distribution's properties:

* Calculate the mean (μ) to find the center.
* Determine the standard deviation (σ) to understand spread.
* Identify any relevant context-specific parameters (like specification limits in manufacturing).
* Consider sample size to decide between normal and t-distribution.

### Step 3: Standardize and Compare

With our distribution characterized, we can put our data into a standard framework:

* Convert raw values to z-scores for comparison.
* Map our specific distribution to the standard normal distribution.
* Identify unusual values or outliers based on standard deviations.
* Compare different groups or samples using standardized measures.

### Step 4: Apply Probability Rules

Now we can use the distribution's properties to make predictions:

* Use the Empirical Rule to understand where most values fall.
* Calculate specific probabilities for ranges of values.
* Set appropriate control limits or decision boundaries.

### Step 5: Make Informed Decisions

Finally, we use our analysis to draw conclusions and take action:

* Compare results to predetermined criteria.
* Make statistical inferences about populations.
* Set practical guidelines based on probabilities.
* Communicate findings and recommendations to stakeholders.
