95%
:   Incorrect. This question tests understanding of the Empirical Rule. The range 9.9mm to 10.1mm represents one standard deviation (0.1mm) below and above the mean (10mm). According to the Empirical Rule, approximately 68% of values fall within ±1 standard deviation of the mean. While 95% falls within ±2 standard deviations and 99.7% within ±3 standard deviations, this question specifically asks about the ±1 standard deviation range.

A student scores 85 on a test where the mean is 75 and the standard deviation is 5. What is their z-score?

1
:   Incorrect. This tests the ability to calculate z-scores, which measure how many standard deviations a value is from the mean. The formula is: z = (x - μ) / σ where x is the value (85), μ is the mean (75), and σ is the standard deviation (5). z = (85 - 75) / 5 = 10 / 5 = 2 This means the score is exactly 2 standard deviations above the mean.

When should you use a t-distribution instead of a normal distribution?

When working with a large sample size (n > 30)
:   Incorrect. This tests understanding of when to apply different types of distributions. The t-distribution is specifically designed for situations with small sample sizes where we don't know the population standard deviation. It has heavier tails than the normal distribution to account for the increased uncertainty in our estimates. With large samples (n > 30), we can typically use the normal distribution due to the Central Limit Theorem. When we know the population standard deviation, we can use the normal distribution regardless of sample size.
