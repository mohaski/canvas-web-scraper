### Terms

The fundamental building blocks include:

* Events - Specific outcomes we want to measure, like a customer making a purchase.
* Sample Space - The complete set of all possible outcomes in a scenario.
* Independent Events - Occurrences that don't influence each other.
* Conditional Probability - The likelihood of one event given another has occurred.
* Prior Probability - Our initial belief about how likely something is to occur.
* Posterior Probability - Our updated belief after seeing new evidence.

### Key Concepts

Understanding probability's role in data science centers on three essential principles:

**First, probability provides the foundation for statistical inference.** When we analyze data samples, probability helps us understand how confident we can be in drawing conclusions about the larger population. This becomes crucial in A/B testing, hypothesis testing, and making business decisions based on data.

**Second, Bayesian reasoning offers a systematic way to update our beliefs as new evidence arrives.** This approach mirrors how humans naturally learn but provides a mathematical framework for doing so rigorously. This becomes particularly powerful in machine learning applications, where we often need to update our models as new data arrives.

**Third, probability theory helps us quantify and manage uncertainty in our predictions and decisions.** This is essential in everything from risk assessment to machine learning model evaluation.
