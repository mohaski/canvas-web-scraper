### Terms Recap

Non-parametric tests are statistical methods that analyze data without assuming a specific underlying distribution. These tests work by examining relative rankings or positions of data points rather than their exact values. Think of them as being like a judge who ranks contestants rather than giving specific scores.

The essential tests we covered include:

* Mann-Whitney U test examines whether one group tends to have higher values than another
* Wilcoxon Signed-Rank test looks at paired differences, like before-and-after measurements
* Kruskal-Wallis test compares three or more groups simultaneously
* Spearman's Rank Correlation measures relationships between variables using rankings
* Fisher's Exact Test calculates precise probabilities for categorical data

### Key Concepts

The power of non-parametric methods lies in their flexibility and robustness.

* Work with ordinal data where we only know the order of values
* Resistant to outliers because they use ranks instead of raw values
* Maintain their validity even when data is skewed or unusually distributed
* Particularly valuable for small sample sizes where we can't rely on large-sample approximations

### Brief Process Overview

The systematic approach to non-parametric testing follows these essential steps:

Step 1: Assessment Phase- First, examine your data's characteristics:

* What type of measurements do you have?
* How many groups are you comparing?
* Are the observations independent or paired?

Step 2: Selection Phase- Choose the appropriate non-parametric test based on:

* Your research question
* Data structure
* Sample size considerations
* Type of comparison needed

Step 3: Implementation Phase- Apply the chosen test while:

* Verifying any required assumptions
* Calculating effect sizes
* Creating appropriate visualizations

Step 4: Interpretation Phase- Understand your results in context:

* What do the rankings tell you about your data?
* How strong is the evidence?
* What are the practical implications?
