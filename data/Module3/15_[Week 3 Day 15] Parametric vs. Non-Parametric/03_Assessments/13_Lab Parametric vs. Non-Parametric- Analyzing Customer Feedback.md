# Lab: Parametric vs. Non-Parametric- Analyzing Customer Feedback

## ![](https://moringa.instructure.com/courses/1426/files/631644/preview?) Lab: Parametric vs. Non-Parametric- Analyzing Customer Feedback

As a junior data analyst at a consumer research firm, you've been tasked with analyzing customer feedback data from a major restaurant chain's menu redesign initiative. The restaurant has implemented new menu items in select locations and needs to understand their impact.

You'll be working with two key datasets:

* Customer satisfaction ratings (1-5 scale) before and after the menu change
* Customer return visit data comparing locations with and without the new menu

Using our four-step process, you'll need to determine whether the menu changes have made a significant impact on customer satisfaction and return rates.

### Instructions

### [Task 1: Assessment Phase](#dpPanel0)

Examine both datasets for:

* Distribution characteristics
* Sample sizes
* Potential outliers
* Create appropriate visualizations

Perform checks of assumptions:

* Normality
* Paired differences normality
* Equal variances

### [Task 2: Selection Phase](#dpPanel1)

Based on your assessment:

* Choose appropriate statistical tests for each analysis
* Justify your choices
* Document any assumptions you're making

### [Task 3: Implementation Phase](#dpPanel2)

* Implement your chosen tests
* Determine significance based on test results

### [Task 4: Interpretation Phase](#dpPanel3)

* Calculate Cohen’s d effect size
* Calculate Cramer’s v effect size
