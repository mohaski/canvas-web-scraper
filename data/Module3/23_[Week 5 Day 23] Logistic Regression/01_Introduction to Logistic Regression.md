Logistic regression is a core technique for modeling binary outcomes—situations where the response variable can take only two possible values, such as "yes/no," "success/failure," or "0/1." As a special case of **Generalized Linear Models (GLMs)**, logistic regression uses the **logit transformation** to link predictors (independent variables) to the probability of a binary outcome.

This module will focus on:

* **Understanding** **logistic regression as a GLM** and its relationship to linear regression.
* **Implementing logistic regression in Python** using real-world data.
* **Exploring** **logistic regression assumptions** and validating them through diagnostics.
* **Evaluating** **model performance using metrics** like the confusion matrix, accuracy score, and classification report.
* **Exploring applications** such as predicting customer churn, medical diagnoses, and fraud detection.

In this module, we look at the special case of **binary logistic regression**. By the end, you will be able to build logistic regression models, interpret their outputs, validate assumptions, and apply diagnostics for robust results—essential skills for real-world data science tasks across industries like healthcare, finance, and marketing.

### Learning Outcomes

After completing this module, you should be able to:

* Explain the significance of binary logistic regression, including the logit transformation, the sigmoid function, and their role in modeling binary classification problems.
* Use Python libraries such as statsmodels and sklearn to build, evaluate, and interpret binary logistic regression models for real-world classification tasks.
* Assess logistic regression models by validating assumptions (e.g., linearity of log-odds, absence of multicollinearity) and interpreting performance metrics such as accuracy, precision, and recall to recommend model improvements.
