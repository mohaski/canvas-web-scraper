### **Scenario:** Predicting Customer Churn in Subscription Services

You're tasked with predicting customer churn at a subscription-based streaming service using binary logistic regression. The model classifies customers as likely to churn (1) or stay (0) based on key features such as tenure, monthly charges, contract type, and number of streams.

#### Steps Overview:

* **Data Preparation:** Load and explore the dataset. Preprocess by one-hot encoding categorical variables and split the data into 70% training and 30% testing sets.
* **Model Training & Interpretation:** Train the logistic regression model. Analyze coefficients to see how each predictor affects churn probability (e.g., higher monthly charges increase churn risk, while longer tenure decreases it).
* **Evaluation:** Evaluate performance with a confusion matrix and metrics like accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score. Check model assumptions using Variance Inflation Factor (VIF) for multicollinearity and Cook’s Distance for outliers.
* **Business Insights:** The model achieves roughly 81% accuracy. High monthly charges are a key churn driver, whereas longer tenure correlates with customer retention. These insights suggest strategies such as offering discounts to high-risk customers and promoting longer-term contracts.

This streamlined process highlights how predictive modeling can drive targeted retention strategies and improve overall customer engagement.
