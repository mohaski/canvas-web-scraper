# Lab: Advanced Tableau Usage

## ![](https://moringa.instructure.com/courses/1426/files/631398/preview?) Lab: Advanced Tableau Usage

You're a junior data analyst at Homes-to-Buy Real Estate Investments, a mid-sized investment firm looking to expand their portfolio into the short-term rental market. The leadership team has tasked you with analyzing your city's Airbnb market to identify profitable investment opportunities and develop a data-driven acquisition strategy.

Homes-to-Buy Real Estate Investments has historically focused on traditional long-term residential and commercial properties. The company recognizes that the short-term rental market represents a significant opportunity for portfolio diversification and potentially higher yields. With $5 million allocated for this initiative, we need to ensure every acquisition decision is backed by robust data analysis.

**Primary Business Questions:**

* **Return on Investment Analysis:** What property characteristics lead to the highest ROI in the short-term rental market?
* **Market Demand Analysis:** Which neighborhoods and property types show consistent demand throughout the year?

### Lab Requirements

For this lab, you will create interactive dashboards to analyze Airbnb listings in a city of your choice to identify investment opportunities and market trends.

1. Create **at least two interconnected dashboards** analyzing:

* Property type performance
* Host performance metrics
* Property feature breakdowns and performance

1. Incorporate **at least one instance** of:

* Parameter creation and interactive use
* Interactive filtering
* Custom dashboard action
* Dashboard navigation

Your dashboards can have a “Home Page” but it does not count as one of the two required dashboards. If you do include a home page ensure proper navigation.  
  
Data should be sourced from Inside Airbnb. It is up to your discretion on which city you would like to choose as well as which data is ultimately incorporated into your analysis. We would recommend starting with the detailed ‘listings’ csv file.

**Key Process Steps:**

* Create parameter controls.
* Create calculated fields to match the parameter controls.
* Create visualizations for respective dashboards.
* Incorporating navigation to switch between dashboards.

#### Step 1: Download and connect to data

* Recommendation: Store data file in its own project folder.
* Choose a city you are invested/interested in.
* Start with the detailed listings data.

#### Step 2: Plan and Create First Dashboard

* Consider making this one a general look at the data, executive summary.  
  + Highlight total numbers, map of listings, different types
* Consider what you can use to create parameter and control selection.
  + Breakdown by neighborhoods, types of properties
  + Metrics like Price per person, estimated monthly revenue
  + Different granular review scores
* Potential seasonal booking trends
* Price distributions
* Occupancy rates

#### Step 3: Plan and Create Second Dashboard

* Deep Dive - breakdown by further information
  + Property types
  + Neighborhoods
  + Amenities/features
  + Host metrics
  + Availability windows
* Consider where to incorporate filtering either for singular or across both dashboards.
* Ensure a consistent store from dashboard 1 to 2.
