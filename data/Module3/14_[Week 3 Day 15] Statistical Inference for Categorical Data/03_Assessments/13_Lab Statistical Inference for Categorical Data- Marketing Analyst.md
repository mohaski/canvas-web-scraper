# Lab: Statistical Inference for Categorical Data- Marketing Analyst

## ![](https://moringa.instructure.com/courses/1426/files/631644/preview?) Lab: Statistical Inference for Categorical Data- Marketing Analyst

You're working as a data analyst for a major retail chain that's analyzing their customer loyalty program. The marketing team wants to understand if there are patterns in how customers engage with their promotional campaigns.

They've collected data on customer responses to different types of promotions (email, SMS, app notification) and whether customers made a purchase within 7 days of receiving the promotion. Additionally they also recorded the time of day when the promotion was opened/accessed and want to know if promotional engagement varies by the time of day.

The marketing team needs your help to:

* Determine if customers show preferences for when they engage with promotions (morning, afternoon, evening)
* Investigate whether the type of promotion is related to customer purchase behavior

This analysis will help the marketing team optimize their promotional strategy and improve campaign effectiveness.

### Instructions

### [Step 1: Define Hypotheses](#dpPanel0)

* Bridge business questions and statistical analysis
* Starting with broad business questions
* Refining them into testable hypotheses

### [Step 2: Data Preparation and Initial Analysis](#dpPanel1)

* Describe basic data characteristics
* Valid and check for missing data
* Create visualizations to understand the basic distribution of your data

### [Step 3: Goodness of Fit Test for Time Preferences](#dpPanel2)

* Analyze whether customers show preferences for promotion timing
* Calculate expected frequencies assuming no time preference
* Perform chi-square goodness of fit test
* Visualize the results

### [Step 4: Test of Independence](#dpPanel3)

* Examine the relationship between promotion type and purchase behavior
* Create a contingency table
* Perform chi-square test of independence
* Visualize the relationship

### [Step 5: Results Interpretation and Recommendations](#dpPanel4)

* Reject the null hypothesis or not
* Determine best results for business
