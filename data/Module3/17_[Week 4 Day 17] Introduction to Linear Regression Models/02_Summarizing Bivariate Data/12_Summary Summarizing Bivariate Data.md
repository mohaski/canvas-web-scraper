### Terms Recap

* **Simple Linear Regression Line (
  ![y With caret equals b 0 plus b 1 x](https://learning.flatironschool.com/equation\_images/%255Chat%257By%257D%2520%253D%2520b\_0%2520%252B%2520b\_1x?scale=1)
  ):** A line that models the relationship between an independent variable (*x*) and a dependent variable (*y*).
* **Predicted Value (
  ![y With caret](https://learning.flatironschool.com/equation\_images/%255Chat%257By%257D?scale=1)
  ):** The estimated value of *y* based on *x*.
* **Intercept (
  ![b 0](https://learning.flatironschool.com/equation\_images/b\_0?scale=1)
  ):** The value of *y* when
  ![x equals 0](https://learning.flatironschool.com/equation\_images/x%2520%253D%25200?scale=1)
  .
* **Slope (
  ![b 1](https://learning.flatironschool.com/equation\_images/b\_1?scale=1)
  ):** The rate of change in *y* for a one-unit change in *x*.
* **Linear Correlation Coefficient (*r*):** A measure of the strength and direction of the linear relationship between *x* and *y*.
* **Scatterplot:** A visual tool to display the relationship between *x* and *y*.

### Key Concepts

* **Visualizing Relationships:** Scatterplots provide an intuitive way to assess patterns, detect outliers, and determine if a linear relationship exists between two variables.
* **Quantifying Relationships:** Linear regression and *r* quantify the strength and direction of the relationship.
  + The regression line allows predictions of *y* based on *x*.
  + The correlation coefficient (*r*) indicates how strong the linear relationship is.
* **Interpreting Results:**  
  + A positive slope (
    ![b 1 greater than 0](https://learning.flatironschool.com/equation\_images/b\_1%2520%253E%25200?scale=1)
    ) indicates that *y* increases as *x* increases.
  + A negative slope (
    ![b 1 less than 0](https://learning.flatironschool.com/equation\_images/b\_1%2520%253C%25200?scale=1)
    ) indicates that *y* decreases as *x* increases.
  + *r* values closer to *1* or *-1* suggest a stronger linear relationship, while
    ![r almost equals 0](https://learning.flatironschool.com/equation\_images/r%2520%255Capprox%25200?scale=1)
    suggests no linear relationship.

### Brief Process Overview

1. **Verify Data Suitability:** Ensure the variables are numeric and appropriate for bivariate analysis.
2. **Define the Variables:** Clearly identify the independent (*x*) and dependent (*y*) variables.
3. **Scatterplot:** Visualize the relationship to detect trends and assess linearity.
4. **Compute the Regression Line and *r*:** If the scatterplot suggests linearity, calculate the regression line (
   ![y With caret equals b 0 plus b 1 x](https://learning.flatironschool.com/equation\_images/%255Chat%257By%257D%2520%253D%2520b\_0%2520%252B%2520b\_1x?scale=1)
   ) and correlation coefficient (*r*).
5. **Interpret Results:** Analyze the slope, intercept, and *r* to provide actionable insights.
