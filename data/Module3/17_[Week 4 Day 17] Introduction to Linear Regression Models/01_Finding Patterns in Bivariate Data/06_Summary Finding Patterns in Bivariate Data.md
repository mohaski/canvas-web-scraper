### Terms Recap

* **Bivariate Data:** Data involving two numeric variables.
* **Independent Variable (*x*):** The predictor or explanatory variable.
* **Dependent Variable (*y*):** The response or outcome variable.
* **Linear Regression Equation:**
  ![y With caret equals b 0 plus b 1 x](https://learning.flatironschool.com/equation\_images/%255Chat%257By%257D%2520%253D%2520b\_0%2520%252B%2520b\_1x?scale=1)
  , where
  ![b 0](https://learning.flatironschool.com/equation\_images/b\_0?scale=1)
  is the intercept and
  ![b 1](https://learning.flatironschool.com/equation\_images/b\_1?scale=1)
  ​ is the slope.
* **Scatter Plot:** A visual representation to observe relationships between *x* and *y*.

### Key Concepts

* The purpose of analyzing bivariate data is to uncover, describe, and quantify the relationship between two numeric variables.
* Linear regression is a theoretical framework used to model this relationship, enabling prediction and insight.
* Scatterplots are a fundamental tool for analyzing bivariate data. They visually represent the relationship between two variables and allow one to see if a linear relationship is plausible

1. Verify Data Suitability.
2. Define the Variables.
