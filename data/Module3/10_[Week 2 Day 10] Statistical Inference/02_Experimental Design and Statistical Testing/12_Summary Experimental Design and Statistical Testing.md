### Terms Recap

Experimental design and statistical testing involve several crucial concepts that work together:

From the **experimental design** side:

* **Control and Treatment Groups**: The baseline and intervention groups
* **Independent and Dependent Variables:** What we manipulate and what we measure
* **Confounding Variables:** External factors we need to control
* **Randomization:** How we assign subjects to groups

From the **statistical testing** side:

* **Hypotheses:** Null and alternative statements we're testing
* **P-values:** Probability of seeing our results by chance
* **Significance Level:** Our threshold for decision making
* **Effect Size:** The magnitude of the difference we observe

### Key Concepts

The most important concepts that unite experimental design and statistical testing are:

* **Evidence-Based Decision Making**: We use carefully designed experiments and rigorous statistical analysis to make decisions rather than relying on intuition. This combines proper experimental controls with appropriate statistical validation.
* **Control and Isolation**: Just as a scientist controls laboratory conditions, we need to isolate the effect we're studying from other influencing factors. This spans both the design phase (controlling variables) and analysis phase (accounting for confounders).
* **Randomization and Balance**: Proper randomization in experimental design helps ensure that any differences between groups are due to chance rather than systematic bias. This creates the foundation for valid statistical testing.
* **Measurement and Precision**: The quality of our experimental design directly affects the reliability of our statistical conclusions. Like using the right tool for measurement, we need appropriate methods to capture our variables of interest.

### Brief Process Overview

The integrated process follows these essential steps:

1. Define research question and hypotheses.
2. Design experimental framework.
3. Determine sample size and power.
4. Implement and collect data.
5. Analyze, test, and interpret.
