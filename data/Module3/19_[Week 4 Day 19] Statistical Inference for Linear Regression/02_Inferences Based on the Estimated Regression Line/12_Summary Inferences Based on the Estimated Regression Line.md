### Terms Recap

* **Confidence Interval for Mean *y* Value:** A range for the average predicted *y* value at a specific *x* value. It estimates the mean response for the population at that *x*.
* **Prediction Interval for Single *y* Value:** A range for an individual predicted *y* value at a specific *x* value. It accounts for both population variability and individual data variability.

### Key Concepts

1. **Confidence Interval for Mean *y* Value:**  
   * Useful for group-level predictions.
   * Helps quantify the uncertainty in the average outcome for a given *x*.
2. **Prediction Interval for Single *y* Value:**  
   * Relevant for individual-level predictions.
   * Accounts for more variability than confidence intervals because it includes both the variability of the mean prediction and individual differences.

### Brief Process Overview

1. **Fit the Regression Model:** Establish the relationship between *x* and *y* using a linear regression model.
2. **Define Target *x* Value(s):** Choose specific *x* value(s) of interest for making predictions.
3. **Calculate Predicted Values:** Use the regression line formula (
   ![ModifyingAbove y With caret equals b 0 plus b 1 x](https://learning.flatironschool.com/equation\_images/%255Chat%257By%257D%2520%253D%2520b\_0%2520%252B%2520b\_1x?scale=1)
   ) to find the predicted *y* values.
4. **Compute Confidence Interval for Mean *y*:** Quantify the uncertainty around the average *y* value for the target *x*.
5. **Compute Prediction Interval for Single *y*:** Account for additional variability in individual outcomes at the same *x*.
6. **Validate Assumptions:** Ensure the model assumptions (linearity, normality, etc.) hold for accurate results.
7. **Interpret Results:** Communicate the meaning and distinctions between confidence and prediction intervals to stakeholders.
