### Terms Recap

* **Slope (
  ![b 1](https://learning.flatironschool.com/equation\_images/b\_1?scale=1)
  ):** The estimated change in the dependent variable (*y*) for a one-unit increase in the independent variable (*x*).
* **Population Slope (
  ![beta 1](https://learning.flatironschool.com/equation\_images/%255Cbeta\_1?scale=1)
  ):** The true relationship between *x* and *y* in the population.
* **Standard Error of the Slope (
  ![SE left parenthesis b 1 right parenthesis](https://learning.flatironschool.com/equation\_images/%255Ctext%257BSE%257D(b\_1)?scale=1)
  ):** Measures the variability of the slope estimate across samples.
* **t-Statistic (*t*):** Quantifies how far the sample slope (
  ![b 1](https://learning.flatironschool.com/equation\_images/b\_1?scale=1)
  ) is from zero (or a hypothesized value) in standard error units.
* **Confidence Interval:** Provides a range within which the true slope (
  ![beta 1](https://learning.flatironschool.com/equation\_images/%255Cbeta\_1?scale=1)
  ) is likely to fall.
* **p-Value:** Indicates the probability of observing a slope as extreme as
  ![b 1](https://learning.flatironschool.com/equation\_images/b\_1?scale=1)
  , assuming the null hypothesis (
  ![upper H 0 colon beta 1 equals 0](https://learning.flatironschool.com/equation\_images/H\_0%253A%2520%255Cbeta\_1%2520%253D%25200?scale=1)
  ) is true.

### Key Concepts

* **Hypothesis Testing for the Slope:**  
  + Tests whether *x* significantly predicts *y*.
  + Rejecting
    ![upper H 0](https://learning.flatironschool.com/equation\_images/H\_0?scale=1)
    (
    ![beta 1 equals 0](https://learning.flatironschool.com/equation\_images/%255Cbeta\_1%2520%253D%25200?scale=1)
    ) suggests a meaningful relationship.
* **Confidence Interval for the Slope:**  
  + Estimates the range of plausible values for
    ![beta 1](https://learning.flatironschool.com/equation\_images/%255Cbeta\_1?scale=1)
    .
  + A narrow interval indicates higher precision in the estimate.
* **Importance of Statistical Inference:**  
  + Confirms whether relationships are statistically significant or due to random chance.
  + Quantifies uncertainty in predictions, building trust in model outcomes.

### Brief Process Overview

1. **Prepare the Data:** Ensure data is clean, numeric, and ready for regression.
2. **Fit the Linear Regression Model:** Calculate the sample slope (
   ![b 1](https://learning.flatironschool.com/equation\_images/b\_1?scale=1)
   ) and intercept (
   ![b 0](https://learning.flatironschool.com/equation\_images/b\_0?scale=1)
   ).
3. **Compute Standard Error:** Quantify the precision of the slope estimate (
   ![SE left parenthesis b 1 right parenthesis](https://learning.flatironschool.com/equation\_images/%255Ctext%257BSE%257D(b\_1)?scale=1)
   ).
4. **Perform Hypothesis Test:** Use the *t*-statistic and p-value to test the significance of
   ![beta 1](https://learning.flatironschool.com/equation\_images/%255Cbeta\_1?scale=1)
   .
5. **Construct Confidence Interval:** Determine a range for the true slope based on the sample slope (
   ![b 1](https://learning.flatironschool.com/equation\_images/b\_1?scale=1)
   ) and standard error (
   ![SE left parenthesis b 1 right parenthesis](https://learning.flatironschool.com/equation\_images/%255Ctext%257BSE%257D(b\_1)?scale=1)
   ).
6. **Interpret Results:** Combine hypothesis test and confidence interval findings to draw meaningful conclusions about *x*'s relationship with *y*.
