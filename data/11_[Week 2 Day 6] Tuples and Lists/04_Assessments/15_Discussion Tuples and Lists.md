# Discussion: Tuples and Lists

## ![](https://moringa.instructure.com/courses/1391/files/620140/preview) Discussion: Tuples and Lists

Welcome to our discussion on Python's tuples and lists! Both of these data structures allow you to store collections of items, but they serve different purposes and have unique characteristics. **Tuples** are immutable, meaning once you create them, you can't change their elements. This makes them great for storing data that shouldn't be modified. **Lists**, on the other hand, are mutable and allow you to add, remove, or change elements, making them more flexible for dynamic data manipulation.
