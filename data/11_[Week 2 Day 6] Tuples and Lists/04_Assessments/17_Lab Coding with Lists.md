# Lab: Coding with Lists

## ![](https://moringa.instructure.com/courses/1391/files/620338/preview?) Lab: Coding with Lists

A **list** in Python is a mutable, ordered collection of elements that can hold items of any data type, including numbers, strings, other lists, or a mix of types. A list's contents can be changed after creation—items can be added, removed, or modified. Because of this flexibility, lists are one of the most commonly used data structures in Python, suitable for storing and managing sequences of items where changes may be needed over time.

**Lists are used when you need a flexible and dynamic way to store and manipulate collections of data.** They are ideal for tasks like accumulating results, keeping track of user inputs, storing items for iteration, or implementing stacks and queues. Lists allow you to sort, slice, filter, and loop through data efficiently, making them well-suited for most general-purpose programming tasks where ordered and changeable collections are needed.

In this lab, you are going to be assessed on your ability to:

* Create lists.
* Print an element in a list by index.
* Add an item to the end of a list.
* Remove an item for the list.
* Iterate over a list.
* Reverse a list.
