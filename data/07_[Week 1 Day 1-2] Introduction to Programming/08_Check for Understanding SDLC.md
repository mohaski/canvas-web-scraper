### Quick Check: Mobile Application

A software development company is considering two different approaches for their upcoming project. The project involves developing a mobile application for a client in the financial industry. The client has provided a general idea of the features they want in the app, but they are open to suggestions and expect the requirements to evolve based on user feedback and market trends.

The company is debating between using the Agile methodology or the Waterfall methodology for this project. They want to choose the approach that will allow them to deliver a high-quality product while adapting to changing requirements and ensuring customer satisfaction.

Which of the following statements best describes the key difference between the Agile and Waterfall methodologies in the context of this scenario?

Agile emphasizes extensive planning and documentation, while Waterfall focuses on flexibility and adaptability.

Agile delivers working software incrementally and values user collaboration, while Waterfall follows a structured and phase-based approach.
:   **Correct:** Agile methodologies embrace an iterative and incremental approach. They focus on delivering working software in short iterations and value close collaboration with customers throughout the development process. Agile teams are adaptive and respond well to changing requirements, as they prioritize user feedback and continuous improvement.

    On the other hand, the Waterfall methodology follows a linear and sequential approach. It consists of distinct phases, such as requirements gathering, design, implementation, testing, and deployment, where each phase must be completed before moving on to the next. Waterfall relies on extensive planning and documentation upfront and assumes that requirements are well-defined and stable.
